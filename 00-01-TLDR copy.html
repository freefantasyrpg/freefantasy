Overview

This system is inspired by AD&D 2e and Basic Fantasy, with additional ideas from The Black Hack, Traveller, and many original mechanics. Most published adventures and monsters are fully compatible with Free Fantasy.

DC 20

To accomplish an action, roll a d20 and add your class bonus, attribute bonus, and any other modifiers. If the total is 20 or higher, you succeed. The only exception is attacking, where you must meet or exceed the target's AC.

A natural 20 is always a success; a natural 1 is always a failure.

Example A trap opens below your feet! Roll d20 + DEX and hope for the best.

Weapon damage

Weapon damage is determined by your class, not by the weapon type.

Example: A fighter deals damage with a d8 — whether using a knife, halberd, whip, or broadsword.

Armor value

Armor comes in three tiers, based solely on price.

Example: Medium armor has AC 14. It may be plate mail, leather armor, or a gambeson — if you paid for medium armor, its AC is 14.

Classes

Each class has a beginner tier (levels 1-3) and several advanced paths (levels 4-10).

Example: An initiate becomes a fighter, berserker, or ranger upon reaching level 4.

Damage and healing

You have endurace, not hit points. After endurance is depleted, you begin losing CON, then STR, then DEX.

Endurace is restored after a short rest. Attributes require magical healing or medical treatment.

Example: You have 4 endurance and CON 11 (+0). After taking 7 damage, your endurance drops to 0 and your CON falls to 8 (-1). A paladin heals you for 3 endurance and 2 CON. You now have 3 of 4 endurance and CON 11 (+0).
