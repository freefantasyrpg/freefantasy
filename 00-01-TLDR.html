TLDR

Overview

Free Fantasy is a class-based fantasy role-playing system inspired by AD&D 2e and Basic Fantasy, incorporating additional mechanics from The Black Hack, Traveller, and original design elements. Most published adventures and bestiaries are compatible without modification.

DC 20 Resolution

To resolve an action, the player rolls 1d20 and applies all relevant class, attribute, and situational modifiers. A result of 20 or higher indicates success.

Attack rolls are resolved against the target's Armor Class (AC). A natural 20 always succeeds. A natural 1 always fails.

Example: A trapdoor opens beneath the character. The player rolls 1d20 + DEX to avoid falling.

Weapon Damage

Weapon damage is determined by the character's class, not by the weapon used. All weapons deal the same damage die for a given class.

Example: A fighter deals 1d8 damage regardless of whether the weapon is a knife, halberd, whip, or broadsword.

Armor Value

Armor is categorized into three tiers, each defined by cost. Each tier provides a fixed Armor Class.

Example: Medium armor provides AC 14. Any armor purchased as medium armor grants AC 14.

Classes

Each class consists of a beginner tier (levels 1-3) and multiple advanced paths (levels 4-10). Upon reaching level 4, the character selects one of the available advanced class options.

Example: An initiate may advance to fighter, berserker, or ranger at level 4.

Damage and Healing

Characters possess Endurance instead of hit points. After Endurance is reduced to 0, damage is applied to attributes in the following order: Constitution (CON), Strength (STR), Dexterity (DEX).

Endurance is fully restored after a short rest. Attribute damage requires magical healing or medical treatment.

Example: A character has 4 Endurance and CON 11 (+0). After taking 7 damage, Endurance is reduced to 0 and CON decreases to 8 (-1). A paladin restores 3 Endurance and 2 CON, returning the character to 3/4 Endurance and CON 11 (+0).
