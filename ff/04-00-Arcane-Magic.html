Arcane magic

Overview

Arcane magic is divided into two categories: Tricks and Powers. Tricks represent minor, effortless magical effects. Powers are potent arcane abilities that carry significant risk when invoked.

Tricks

Tricks are simple arcane effects that require minimal effort and pose no danger to the caster. A Trick cannot fail and never triggers magical backlash.

Risk Levels

Each Power has an inherent risk value based on its potency:

  • Minor Powers ⇒ Risk 5
  • Major Powers ⇒ Risk 10

Powers

Powers are structured arcane workings that channel substantial magical force. Using a Power requires a risk roll:

d20 + INT bonus + Class bonus - Risk Note: The INT bonus might be negative.

If the final result is 20 or higher, the Power is successfully cast. If the result is below 20, the Power fails and the caster suffers magical backlash as determined by the GM.

The higher the risk, the more difficult the Power is to control. Class bonuses and high Intelligence can offset this danger, but even experienced casters must treat major Powers with caution.

Backlash

When a Power fails, the caster may suffer arcane backlash. Backlash effects can include temporary attribute loss, exhaustion, magical disorientation, or unintended spell effects. The exact consequences are determined by the GM and the severity of the failed Power.

Learning Magic

All arcane casters begin with a small number of Tricks. Minor Powers and Major Powers are typically learned at higher levels or through special training, rituals, or ancient tomes.

Using Magic in Play

Tricks are ideal for utility, exploration, and minor problem-solving. Powers are intended for dramatic moments, combat, and significant narrative impact. Casters must weigh the benefits of a Power against the potential consequences of failure.

Result

d20 Result
30 or more Legendärer success: The spell succeeds and two additional bonuses
25 - 29 Critical success: The spell succeeds and receives an additional bonus.
20 - 24 Normal success: The spell works as described.
11 - 19 Simple fail: The energy fizzles out. Nothing happens, but the spell is blocked for that day (until the next long rest).
2 - 10 Blunder: The magic goes wrong. Roll immediately on the mishap table.
Natural 1 Catastrophe: Regardless of modifiers, something terribly unpleasant happens, which the GM improvises spontaneously!

Mishap (2 - 10)

d10 Effect Explanation
1 Arcane antilight All lights, arcane or not, torches, and candles in a 20m radius are extinguished.
2 Magic echo A deafening sound. Communication is impossible for 1d4 minutes.
3 Teleport blunder The caster is teleported a short distance, 1d6m, in a random direction. In a fight the target is 1-4 in the same zone, 5-6 in another zone.
4 Energy transfer The caster all nearby energy. Everyone near the caster or in the same zone 1d4 cold damage.
5 Verbal blockage The caster's tongue grows 4x the size for the next 1d4 hours. Speaking and casting is impossible.
6 Mental exhaustion A terrible headache! Everyone near the caster or in the same zone roll their checks at -2 for the rest of the day.
7 Wrong target The spell affects the wrong target.
8 Detonation 1d6 damage for everyone near the caster or in the same zone.
9 Arcane vampirism The caster loses 1d6 CON*. This is the normal attribute damage, ignoring the current endurance.
10 Crack in the fabric An extra planar enemy suddenly spawns and attacks the caster.
