Roll 20 or higher

Overview

In this system, all non-attack checks use a fixed Difficulty Class of 20. Only attack rolls target Armor Class (AC); everything else—saving throws, spellcasting checks, skill attempts, and thief abilities—must meet or exceed DC 20 to succeed. This creates a unified, easy-to-remember resolution mechanic.

The DC 20 system establishes a single, universal Difficulty Class for all actions outside of combat attacks. Whenever a character attempts something risky, uncertain, or extraordinary—whether resisting danger with a saving throw, casting a spell under pressure, picking a lock, disarming a trap, sneaking past a guard, or performing any other specialized skill—they must roll a check against DC 20.

Attack rolls are the only exception. Instead of using DC 20, attacks are resolved by rolling against the target’s Armor Class (AC). All other checks ignore AC entirely and rely on the fixed DC.

To add depth and reward exceptional performance, the system includes two higher success thresholds:

  • Result 25: The character achieves a bonus effect. This represents an above-average success—cleaner execution, faster completion, reduced risk, or a small additional benefit depending on the action.
  • Result 30 or higher: The character gains a special bonus effect. This reflects an extraordinary success—masterful execution, significant advantage, or a powerful narrative impact determined by the GM.

This approach simplifies gameplay by removing variable difficulty ratings. Players always know what they need to achieve, and the GM can focus on narrative consequences rather than adjusting numbers. A DC 20 represents a meaningful challenge: success requires competence, luck, or both, making every roll feel significant. The system is easy to teach, fast to use, and consistent across all character types and situations.
