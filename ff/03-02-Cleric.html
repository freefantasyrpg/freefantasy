Acolyte

Levels: 1 - 3

Cleric Apprentices are newly initiated servants of their faith, still learning the rites, traditions, and responsibilities of divine service. They possess only basic knowledge of prayers, blessings, and sacred rituals. Their connection to the divine is genuine but not yet fully developed, and their miracles are modest in scope. Apprentices rely on discipline, humility, and guidance from senior clergy. Though inexperienced, they are devoted, steadfast, and eager to prove their worth through service and compassion.

Cleric

Levels: 4 - 10

lerics are fully ordained members of their religious order, empowered to channel divine energy with confidence and authority. Their prayers can heal, protect, or repel supernatural threats, and their presence often inspires courage in allies. Clerics uphold sacred traditions, act as moral guides, and serve as intermediaries between mortals and the divine. Their training grants them resilience, discipline, and a deep understanding of holy rites. Whether in temples or on the battlefield, clerics stand as unwavering pillars of faith.

Overview

Requirements

  • Attributes: Wisdom 15, Charisma 13

Skills and powers

  • Weapons: Blunt
  • Armor: Light armor (AC 12)
  • Level 4: Turn undead
  • Level 7: Divine shield Once per encounter, you and all allies within 10m gain +2 Defense for one round. This stacks with armor.
  • Level 10: Greater Healing Your Heal ability increases to 2d6 endurance and 4 attribute points (level times per day). On a roll of 25+, the healing also removes poison or disease. On 30+, it restores a lost limb or crippling injury.

Druid

Levels: 4 - 10

Druids are guardians of nature and keepers of ancient, primal wisdom. They draw power from forests, rivers, and the living world, shaping it through rituals, chants, and deep spiritual connection. Druids protect natural places, heal wounded creatures, and maintain balance between civilization and wilderness. Their abilities often reflect the environment they serve—subtle, adaptive, and rooted in harmony. Calm yet formidable, druids embody the quiet strength of the natural world and act as its voice when threatened.

Overview

Requirements

  • Attributes: Constitution 12, Wisdom 15, Charisma 13

Skills and powers

  • Weapons: Blunt
  • Armor: Light armor (AC 12)
  • Level 4: Shapeshifting Man-sized, e.g. Bear, Shark, Dolphin, Lion. Only body changes, not armor, inventory, or weapon.
  • Level 7: Nature’s Wrath Once per encounter, vines, roots, or wind lash out at enemies within 10m. Targets must make a saving throw (DC 20) or suffer 2d6 damage and -2 to attack for one round.
  • Level 10: Spirit of the Wild Once per day you gain +2 to all Wisdom-based checks, +2 Defense, +2d6 bonus damage on your next attack (claw, staff, or natural strike)

Paladin

Levels: 4 - 10

Paladins are holy warriors bound by sacred oaths and unwavering conviction. They combine martial skill with divine authority, standing as champions of justice, protection, and righteousness. Their faith grants them resilience against corruption and the ability to channel holy power in battle. Paladins act with honor and discipline, serving as shields for the innocent and scourges of evil. Their presence on the battlefield is both inspiring and intimidating, for they fight not only with steel, but with purpose.

Overview

Requirements
